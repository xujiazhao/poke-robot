Our Belief

The iPhone moment
for robotics.

Every great product category has a moment when it stops being a novelty and becomes a necessity. For home robots, that moment hasn't arrived — yet.

01

Form is intention

We chose a humanoid form — not to impress, but because a home deserves a presence, not a appliance. If it looks like a being, it carries emotional meaning.

02

Utility is respect

Real tasks, real help. No choreographed demos, no scripted performances. A robot that works is a robot that respects your time and intelligence.

03

Personality is connection

Simulated personality isn't deception — it's the bridge that lets humans and robots coexist naturally. Warmth isn't a feature. It's a design principle.

"We don't build robots that perform.
We build robots that belong."
